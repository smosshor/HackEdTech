EdTech 101

Problem Space

Online learning and educational tools are increasing part of how students of all ages learn new skills. Yet learning through technology offers unique challenges in design, implementation and evaluation.

Duolingo

Problem: Learning a language is difficult to do in a static way without talking to people who speak the language

Solution: Interactive, community centered, personalized learning platform to learn languages with lots of gamification

Hack112: Education Ideas

Problem Space

Educational games or projects do not have to bring any crazy new revolutionary way of learning to the field, they just need to have some kind of educational component to them that brings about real learning. Nearly everything can become educational with the right twist, so do not be discouraged if you do not believe you have the right mind for education. Ask friends or mentors, think of cool ideas, and keep education in mind! Here are some starter ideas with the technology available to you at HACK112 in mind! Enjoy!

Kinect

    Virtual Mad Libs (ex. With history, storytelling). Interact with the environment and depending on what action you do, a certain word will fill the space, in Mad Lib form.
    Expand on physical education and interact with the user possibly like wii fit!

3D

    Virtual personal classroom; Expand on how a kid would interact in a classroom setting by implementing a number of different environments that could affect how a child learns.
    Use 3D capabilities to maneuver through space and create a sense of wonder and excited while teaching about our solar system and beyond.

Websites

    Websites or LMS that does blackboard/ piazza better for a specific use case
    Creating a database or ‘community’ for educational groups

Open CV

    Building sentences, doing math, or creating anything that could interactively help a child learn.

Tkinter

    Simple but unique games in the fields of: addition, subtraction, multiplication, division
    Representing how circuits, electric fields, or simple physics works by creating your own physics engine!
    Use a basic concept of machine learning to teach kids how to recognize and write certain numbers or shapes or anything else!
